Sinigang

Sinigang is a popular Filipino soup known for its savory and tangy flavor, traditionally made with tamarind as the souring agent. This comforting dish typically includes pork, shrimp, or fish, simmered with vegetables like radish, eggplant, okra, and water spinach. Its signature sour broth, often flavored with tamarind, gives it a refreshing and appetizing taste that pairs perfectly with steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • Protein: pork, shrimp, or fish
  • Veggies: tomato, okra, onion, radish, eggplant, etc.
  • Fish sauce (patis)
  • Water
  • Water spinnach

Instructions

  1. Sauté aromatics: In a pot, sauté chopped onions and tomatoes until softened.
  2. Add meat and simmer: Add pork (or other protein) and cook until browned. Pour in water and simmer until the meat is tender.
  3. Add vegetables and souring agent: Add vegetables like radish, eggplant, and okra. Stir in tamarind (or sinigang mix) to taste.
  4. Season and finish: Add fish sauce, salt, and pepper to season. Add leafy greens last (like kangkong), simmer briefly, then serve hot with rice.
